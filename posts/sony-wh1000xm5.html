Sony WH-1000XM5 — Best Noise Cancelling Headphones for UAE

Industry-leading ANC, 30-hour battery life and a refined design that makes long listening sessions effortless.

Why the XM5 Stands Out

The Sony WH-1000XM5 is widely considered the benchmark for wireless noise cancelling headphones. The eighth-generation processor uses eight microphones to cancel ambient noise — from aircraft cabin hum to the constant background of Dubai's traffic and busy malls.

Sound quality is balanced and detailed out of the box, with a warm low end that works well for everything from Arabic pop to podcast binges. The LDAC codec support means you get hi-res audio over Bluetooth if your phone supports it.

Comfort & Build

At 250g, these are noticeably lighter than the XM4. The headband distributes pressure evenly, and the ear cushions use soft synthetic leather that doesn't trap heat as much — important when you're outdoors in UAE summers.

The folding mechanism is gone (they only lay flat now), which means the carrying case is slightly larger. A minor trade-off for the improved fit.

Battery & Features

  • 30 hours of playback with ANC on — easily a week of daily commuting
  • 3-minute quick charge gives 3 hours of playback
  • Multipoint connection — pair with phone and laptop simultaneously
  • Speak-to-Chat — music pauses automatically when you talk
  • USB-C charging and 3.5mm wired option included

Who Should Buy

If you commute on the Dubai Metro, work in open-plan offices, or fly frequently between UAE and other Gulf states, the XM5 is the best investment you can make in personal audio. The noise cancellation alone justifies the price.

If you primarily want earbuds for gym or running, consider the AirPods Pro 2 or Samsung Galaxy Buds2 Pro instead — the XM5 are over-ear and not designed for workouts.

Practical Notes for UAE

The touch controls on the right ear cup can be overly sensitive in high humidity. Sony's Headphones Connect app lets you adjust sensitivity or switch to button-only controls if this bothers you.

For calls, the XM5 performs well indoors but struggles with wind noise outdoors — keep that in mind if you take calls walking between buildings.
